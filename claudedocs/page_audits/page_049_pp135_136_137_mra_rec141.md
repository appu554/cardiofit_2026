## Source PDF Content

**Practice Point 1.3.5 (eGFR Dip Tolerance):**
- "A reversible decrease in eGFR with commencement of SGLT2i treatment may occur and is generally not an indication to discontinue therapy"
- Post hoc EMPA-REG + CREDENCE: eGFR drop ≥10% NOT associated with increased risk
- CREDENCE: eGFR drop ≥30% uncommon (4%) but associated with modestly increased kidney adverse events
- **Key threshold: "tolerate acute eGFR decrease of ≤30% with initiation"**
- If >30% decline: check for hypovolemia, adjust diuretic, discontinue nephrotoxic agents, evaluate alternatives

**Practice Point 1.3.6 (Continue Below eGFR 20):**
- "Once SGLT2i initiated, reasonable to continue even if eGFR falls below 20 ml/min per 1.73 m²"
- "Unless it is not tolerated or kidney replacement therapy is initiated"
- Trial protocols (CREDENCE, DAPA-CKD) specified continuation below enrollment threshold
- "Reasonable to discontinue SGLT2i prior to initiation of kidney replacement therapy"

**Practice Point 1.3.7 (Transplant Exclusion):**
- "SGLT2i have not been adequately studied in kidney transplant recipients"
- "Immunosuppressed and potentially at increased risk for infections"
- "Recommendation to use SGLT2i does not apply to kidney transplant recipients"

**Research Recommendations:**
- Long-term (>5 years) safety/efficacy studies needed
- SGLT2i in T1D for kidney/heart protection
- SGLT2i for CKD G5 and eGFR <20 or dialysis patients
- SGLT2i for transplant recipients
- Cost-effectiveness analysis
- Implementation in clinical practice

**Section 1.4 — Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonists (MRA):**

**Recommendation 1.4.1 (2A — Weak/Moderate):**
- "We suggest a nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist with proven kidney or cardiovascular benefit for patients with T2D, an eGFR ≥25 ml/min per 1.73 m², normal serum potassium concentration, and albuminuria (≥30 mg/g [≥3 mg/mmol]) despite maximum tolerated dose of RAS inhibitor"
- Based on FIDELIO-DKD and FIGARO-DKD (finerenone trials)
- Finerenone slows eGFR loss + decreases CV event risk
- Hyperkalemia risk noted with potassium monitoring required
- Lack of data combining nonsteroidal MRA + SGLT2i
