### Cross-Domain Patterns We've Enabled — The Ones No Other Platform Has


**Pattern 1 (Operational):** Renal gating blocks a card that therapeutic inertia would otherwise generate. Patient has HbA1c 8.5 for 6 months with no medication change — inertia detector fires. But eGFR is 22 and the recommended next step is SGLT2i. Renal gate blocks: "Cannot initiate SGLT2i below eGFR 20." The system doesn't just flag inertia — it explains *why* the physician may not have intensified: the obvious next step is contraindicated. This prevents false-positive inertia alerts and instead generates: "Glycaemic inertia detected. Standard next step (SGLT2i) is contraindicated at current eGFR. Consider alternative: DPP4i with renal dose adjustment."


**Pattern 2 (Operational):** Masked HTN reclassifies inertia severity. Patient appears controlled in clinic (128/78). Inertia detector sees no BP inertia (clinic BP at target). But masked HTN classifier reveals home BP is 152/94. Now the inertia detector should retroactively fire for hemodynamic inertia — the patient has been above home BP target for weeks, but clinic visits showed "controlled." This cross-reference transforms a "no inertia" assessment into "masked inertia" — the most dangerous kind because the physician believes therapy is working.


**Pattern 3 (Operational):** CGM trajectory divergence detects medication-timing mismatch. CGM analytics shows TIR improving over 14 days (medication working). But domain decomposition shows cardio domain declining simultaneously. Divergence detector fires: "Glucose improving while cardio declining." Meanwhile, masked HTN classifier shows morning BP is 20 mmHg higher than evening. Medication timing hypothesis: the patient takes their antihypertensive in the morning (covering the clinic visit window) but their antidiabetic in the evening (covering overnight glucose). The glucose medication works because it covers the right window. The BP medication appears to work (clinic visit in afternoon, peak effect) but doesn't cover the morning surge. Cross-domain reasoning produces: "Consider switching antihypertensive to bedtime dosing."


**Pattern 4 (Enabled by stability check — what we're about to build):** Cluster flapping reveals unstable clinical phenotype. Patient oscillates between Cluster 3 (insulin-resistant, high variability) and Cluster 5 (well-managed, stable) across successive batch runs. The stability check detects this oscillation. But *why* is the patient oscillating? Cross-referencing with domain decomposition reveals: glucose domain oscillates (CGM TIR swings 45-70% between sensor cycles), while cardio and body comp are stable. The cluster flapping is driven by a single unstable domain, not genuine phenotype change. The system should: (a) hold the patient in their current cluster (stability policy), (b) flag the glucose domain instability as the root cause, (c) generate a card: "Patient's phenotype classification is unstable due to glucose variability — stabilize glycaemic control before reassigning therapy pathway."


**Pattern 5 (Enabled by stability check):** Cluster transition validates therapy response. Patient was in Cluster 2 (concordant deterioration, high CKM velocity) for 8 weeks. SGLT2i was added. After 6 weeks, the next clustering run moves them to Cluster 4 (improving trajectory, dual-domain benefit). The stability check sees this as a genuine transition (not flapping) because: (a) it's a single directional move, (b) it coincides with a medication change in the IOR timeline, (c) the domain decomposition confirms improvement in both glucose and cardio domains. The system generates: "Phenotype transition Cluster 2 → Cluster 4 following SGLT2i initiation — therapy response confirmed. Continue current regimen."


**Pattern 6 (Enabled by stability check):** Cluster stability predicts inertia safety. When the inertia detector flags "no medication change for 16 weeks" but the patient's cluster assignment has been stable in a good-outcome cluster for 12+ weeks, the inertia alert should be deprioritized. Stable phenotype + stable cluster = the current regimen is maintaining the patient. Inertia in this context is appropriate therapeutic maintenance, not clinical negligence. The stability check provides the evidence to distinguish "justified stability" from "dangerous inertia."

### What Becomes Possible After Phenotype Stability


The stability check is the last piece that connects the clustering pipeline to the clinical reasoning layer. Without stability, clusters are batch outputs that overwrite KB-20 state without context. With stability, clusters become longitudinal clinical narratives — a patient's phenotype trajectory over months tells a story about their disease progression and therapy response that no single snapshot provides.


This is the transition from **phenotype classification** (what cluster is this patient in today?) to **phenotype dynamics** (how has this patient moved through phenotype space over time, and what does that trajectory predict?). It's the difference between a photograph and a movie.

# Phenotype Clustering Temporal Stability Check — Implementation Plan


## Clinical Rationale


**Why cluster stability is a clinical requirement, not just a data quality concern:**


The phenotype clustering pipeline (V4 Task 13) runs UMAP→HDBSCAN on 21 features, assigns each patient a cluster label (e.g., CLUSTER_3), and PATCHes the assignment into KB-20 via `/api/v1/patient/:id/v4-state`. The therapy mapper then associates each cluster with a therapeutic pathway (INTENSIVE_DUAL_DOMAIN_ADHERENCE, CONSERVATIVE_DEPRESCRIBING, LIFESTYLE_FIRST_AGGRESSIVE, etc.). Decision cards reference the cluster to contextualize recommendations ("patients like you in Cluster 3 on SGLT2i showed median HbA1c delta of -0.9 at 12 weeks").


The problem is temporal instability. HDBSCAN is a density-based algorithm — patients near cluster boundaries can oscillate between clusters across successive batch runs due to minor changes in the input feature space. A patient whose glucose domain swings between TIR 48% and 52% might alternate between a "deteriorating" cluster and a "stable" cluster, even though the clinical difference is negligible. This creates three specific harms:


**Harm 1 — Therapy pathway whiplash.** If Cluster 3 maps to "INTENSIVE_DUAL_DOMAIN_ADHERENCE" and Cluster 5 maps to "MAINTENANCE_OPTIMIZATION," a flapping patient alternates between being told to intensify and being told to maintain. The physician sees contradictory recommendations week over week, loses trust in the system, and ignores future recommendations — even when they're correct.


**Harm 2 — IOR evidence contamination.** The similar-patient query in the IOR system filters by phenotype cluster. If a patient flaps between clusters, their intervention-outcome records are split across clusters — half in Cluster 3, half in Cluster 5. Neither cluster has a complete outcome history for the patient, and the aggregate statistics for each cluster are contaminated by a patient who doesn't stably belong to either.


**Harm 3 — False phenotype transitions masking real ones.** If the system doesn't distinguish flapping from genuine transitions, it can't detect when a patient truly changes phenotype (e.g., from insulin-resistant to well-managed after SGLT2i initiation). The signal (genuine transition driven by therapy response) is drowned in noise (boundary oscillation driven by measurement variability). The clinician needs to know: "This patient moved from Cluster 2 to Cluster 4 — this is real, caused by SGLT2i response" vs. "This patient appeared in Cluster 4 this week but was in Cluster 2 last week — this is noise, ignore it."


**What the clustering literature says about temporal stability:**


Von Luxburg et al. (2010, Statistics and Computing) established that clustering stability — the consistency of cluster assignments across perturbations of the data — is a fundamental quality criterion for any clustering that will be used for downstream decisions. Unstable assignments indicate that the clustering is fitting noise rather than structure.


For clinical phenotyping specifically, Ahlqvist et al. (2018) noted in their diabetes subtyping work that 15-20% of patients at cluster boundaries reclassified across bootstrap resamples. They addressed this with a confidence score (posterior probability of cluster membership) and excluded low-confidence patients from subgroup analyses. The CardioFit pipeline needs an equivalent mechanism.


Hennig (2007, Journal of Classification) proposed the "dissolution point" concept — the minimum perturbation needed to dissolve a cluster assignment. Patients with low dissolution points are unstable by construction. The practical implementation is a membership probability or distance-to-centroid metric that quantifies how firmly a patient belongs to their assigned cluster.


**The specific stability mechanisms required:**


1. **Membership confidence score:** For each patient-cluster assignment, compute a confidence score reflecting how firmly the patient belongs. HDBSCAN natively provides membership probabilities (`probabilities_` attribute) — patients with probability <0.5 are boundary cases. Additionally, compute distance-to-assigned-centroid vs. distance-to-nearest-other-centroid — the ratio indicates separability.


2. **Minimum dwell policy:** A patient stays in their assigned cluster for a configurable minimum period (default 4 weeks) unless a qualifying override event occurs. This prevents weekly flapping while allowing genuine transitions triggered by clinical events.


3. **Override event detection:** Certain clinical events should bypass the dwell policy and allow immediate reclassification: MHRI category crossing (OPTIMAL→MODERATE), CKM stage transition, medication class addition/removal, hospitalization event, or domain decomposition showing concordant deterioration. These are "genuine phenotype-altering events" that warrant reclassification even within the dwell period.


4. **Transition classification:** When a patient's cluster assignment changes, classify the transition as: GENUINE_TRANSITION (directional, coincides with clinical event, confirmed by domain decomposition), PROBABLE_FLAP (oscillation between same two clusters, no clinical event, boundary confidence), or UNCERTAIN (insufficient history to classify).


5. **Cluster trajectory tracking:** Store the sequence of cluster assignments over time. A patient's cluster trajectory (Cluster 2 × 12 weeks → Cluster 4 × 8 weeks → Cluster 4 ongoing) is itself a clinical narrative that feeds into IOR similar-patient queries and therapy response evaluation.


6. **Flap dampening:** When a patient is detected as flapping (≥2 oscillations between the same pair of clusters within 3 months), freeze their assignment at the more clinically conservative cluster (the one that triggers more monitoring, not less) and flag for manual phenotype review.


---


**Cross-domain integration points unique to stability:**


**Stability × Inertia Detection:** A patient stable in a well-managed cluster for 3+ months, with clinical targets met, should suppress inertia alerts. "No medication change for 4 months" is appropriate maintenance, not inertia, when the phenotype is stable-good. Conversely, a patient stable in a deteriorating cluster for 3+ months with no medication change is *confirmed* inertia — the stability proves it's not a transient state.


**Stability × IOR Evidence Quality:** When querying similar-patient outcomes, filter by patients with stable cluster membership (≥4 weeks in the query cluster). Flapping patients contaminate aggregate statistics and should be excluded or down-weighted. This directly improves the quality of IOR insight summaries on decision cards.


**Stability × Domain Decomposition:** When a cluster transition occurs, the domain decomposition reveals which domain drove the transition. If the patient moved from Cluster 2 (high-risk) to Cluster 4 (improving) and the domain decomposition shows cardio domain improved while glucose stayed flat, the transition was driven by BP improvement — possibly validating an antihypertensive change. This feedback loop connects clustering → domain trajectory → therapy validation.


**Stability × Engagement Context:** A patient whose cluster assignment changes coincident with an engagement collapse (Module 9 RED, MEASUREMENT_AVOIDANT) may have changed cluster because their *data* changed (fewer readings, different measurement context), not because their *phenotype* changed. The stability system should flag: "Cluster transition coincides with engagement collapse — reassignment may reflect data quality change, not clinical change. Hold previous cluster pending engagement recovery."


---


**India-Specific Stability Considerations:**


- **Seasonal feature variation:** Indian patients show seasonal variation in BMI (festival weight gain), glucose (Ramadan/Diwali dietary changes), and BP (heat-related variation). Batch runs in different seasons will produce different feature vectors for the same patient, causing apparent cluster transitions that are seasonal, not clinical. The stability system should track seasonal context and extend the dwell period during known seasonal variation windows.


- **Intermittent CGM users:** When a patient uses CGM for 2 weeks (features include TIR, CV, GMI) and then reverts to SMBG (features fall back to FBG/HbA1c only), the feature vector changes dramatically — not because the patient changed, but because the data source changed. This is a **data modality transition**, not a phenotype transition. The stability system must detect data modality shifts and handle them as missing-data imputation rather than feature change.


**Australia-Specific Stability Considerations:**


- **Remote community batch timing:** Aboriginal community health data may arrive in bursts after connectivity is restored. Batch clustering runs that coincide with data bursts may show rapid apparent transitions for remote patients. The stability system should weight recent data availability when assessing transition genuineness.


- **GPMP review cycle alignment:** The 6-month GPMP review is a natural point for phenotype reassessment. Cluster assignments that change at GPMP review time should be flagged as "REVIEW_ALIGNED" — the GP can use the cluster transition as part of the chronic disease management review.

## Architecture Overview


The stability system sits between the clustering pipeline (batch Python) and KB-20 (state persistence):


```
Clustering Pipeline (Python batch)
    ├── UMAP → HDBSCAN → cluster labels + probabilities
    ├── Centroid computation + therapy mapping
    └── Raw assignments + confidence scores
              │
              ▼
    Stability Engine (Go, KB-20 adjacent)
    ├── Load patient's cluster history from DB
    ├── Compute transition classification (genuine/flap/uncertain)
    ├── Apply dwell policy (min 4 weeks unless override)
    ├── Check override events (MHRI crossing, CKM transition, med change, etc.)
    ├── Cross-reference domain decomposition for driver identification
    ├── Cross-reference engagement for data quality context
    ├── Compute final stable assignment + confidence
    └── Persist stable assignment + transition record
              │
              ▼
    KB-20 PATCH /v4-state
    ├── phenotype_cluster: "CLUSTER_4" (stable assignment, not raw)
    ├── phenotype_confidence: 0.82
    ├── phenotype_stable_since: "2026-02-15"
    └── phenotype_transition_pending: false
```


**The critical design decision:** The clustering pipeline produces *raw* assignments. The stability engine produces *stable* assignments. Only stable assignments are written to KB-20 and consumed by downstream systems (IOR queries, card generation, therapy mapping). Raw assignments are stored in a history table for audit and stability analysis but never directly consumed for clinical decisions.

```go
// kb-20-patient-profile/internal/models/cluster_history.go
package models


import "time"


// ClusterAssignmentRecord stores each raw cluster assignment from the batch pipeline.
type ClusterAssignmentRecord struct {
    ID                string    `gorm:"type:uuid;primaryKey;default:gen_random_uuid()" json:"id"`
    PatientID         string    `gorm:"size:100;index;not null" json:"patient_id"`
    RunID             string    `gorm:"size:100;index;not null" json:"run_id"`         // batch pipeline run identifier
    RunDate           time.Time `gorm:"not null" json:"run_date"`
    RawClusterLabel   string    `gorm:"size:30;not null" json:"raw_cluster_label"`     // raw HDBSCAN assignment
    MembershipProb    float64   `json:"membership_probability"`                        // HDBSCAN probability
    DistToCentroid    float64   `json:"distance_to_centroid"`                          // distance to assigned centroid
    DistToNearest     float64   `json:"distance_to_nearest_other"`                     // distance to nearest OTHER centroid
    SeparabilityRatio float64   `json:"separability_ratio"`                            // dist_nearest / dist_own (>2 = well-separated)
    IsNoise           bool      `json:"is_noise"`                                      // HDBSCAN label -1
    StableCluster     string    `gorm:"size:30" json:"stable_cluster"`                 // the assignment after stability processing
    WasOverridden     bool      `json:"was_overridden"`                                // true if stability engine changed the raw assignment
    OverrideReason    string    `gorm:"size:50" json:"override_reason,omitempty"`
    CreatedAt         time.Time `json:"created_at"`
}


// ClusterTransitionRecord logs each time a patient's stable cluster changes.
type ClusterTransitionRecord struct {
    ID                  string    `gorm:"type:uuid;primaryKey;default:gen_random_uuid()" json:"id"`
    PatientID           string    `gorm:"size:100;index;not null" json:"patient_id"`
    TransitionDate      time.Time `gorm:"not null" json:"transition_date"`
    PreviousCluster     string    `gorm:"size:30;not null" json:"previous_cluster"`
    NewCluster          string    `gorm:"size:30;not null" json:"new_cluster"`
    TransitionType      string    `gorm:"size:30;not null" json:"transition_type"`      // GENUINE, FLAP_DAMPENED, OVERRIDE, INITIAL
    Classification      string    `gorm:"size:30;not null" json:"classification"`       // GENUINE_TRANSITION, PROBABLE_FLAP, UNCERTAIN, DWELL_HELD
    DwellDaysInPrevious int       `json:"dwell_days_in_previous"`
    ConfidenceInNew     float64   `json:"confidence_in_new"`


    // Cross-domain context at transition
    TriggerEvent        string    `gorm:"size:50" json:"trigger_event,omitempty"`        // what triggered the transition
    DominantDomainDriver string   `gorm:"size:20" json:"dominant_domain_driver,omitempty"` // which MHRI domain drove it
    MHRICategoryAtTransition string `gorm:"size:20" json:"mhri_category_at_transition,omitempty"`
    EngagementStatusAtTransition string `gorm:"size:10" json:"engagement_status_at_transition,omitempty"`
    DataModalityChange  bool      `json:"data_modality_change"`                          // CGM started/stopped


    CreatedAt           time.Time `json:"created_at"`
}


// PatientClusterState is the current stability state for a patient.
type PatientClusterState struct {
    PatientID            string     `json:"patient_id"`
    CurrentStableCluster string     `json:"current_stable_cluster"`
    StableSince          time.Time  `json:"stable_since"`
    DwellDays            int        `json:"dwell_days"`
    Confidence           float64    `json:"confidence"`
    PendingRawCluster    *string    `json:"pending_raw_cluster,omitempty"`   // raw assignment that differs but is being held
    PendingSince         *time.Time `json:"pending_since,omitempty"`
    FlapCount            int        `json:"flap_count"`                     // oscillations in last 90 days
    IsFlapping           bool       `json:"is_flapping"`                    // ≥2 oscillations between same pair
    FlapPair             []string   `json:"flap_pair,omitempty"`            // the two clusters being flapped between
    TransitionHistory    []ClusterTransitionRecord `json:"transition_history,omitempty"`
}


// StabilityDecision is the output of the stability engine for a single patient-run evaluation.
type StabilityDecision struct {
    PatientID           string  `json:"patient_id"`

    Confidence          float64 `json:"confidence"`
    TransitionType      string  `json:"transition_type,omitempty"`
    TriggerEvent        string  `json:"trigger_event,omitempty"`
    DomainDriver        string  `json:"domain_driver,omitempty"`
}


// OverrideEvent represents a clinical event that can bypass the dwell policy.

    EventType string    `json:"event_type"`
    EventDate time.Time `json:"event_date"`
    Domain    string    `json:"domain,omitempty"`

}
```

```yaml
# Minimum dwell policy
dwell:

  - event_type: "HOSPITALIZATION"

  - event_type: "CONCORDANT_DETERIORATION"

  - event_type: "CGM_INITIATION"

transition_classification:

    requires_clinical_event: true        # must coincide with override event
    min_confidence_in_new: 0.5           # new cluster confidence ≥0.5
```

```go
// kb-20-patient-profile/internal/services/phenotype_stability.go
package services


import (
    "fmt"
    "time"


    "kb-patient-profile/internal/models"
)


type StabilityConfig struct {
    MinDwellWeeks          int
    ExtendedDwellWeeks     int
    FlapLookbackDays       int
    MinOscillationsForFlap int
    HighMembershipProb     float64
    ModerateMembershipProb float64
    CGMStartGraceWeeks     int
    CGMStopGraceWeeks      int
    ConservatismRank       map[string]int // cluster → rank (lower = more conservative)
}


type StabilityInput struct {
    PatientID            string
    RawCluster           string
    MembershipProb       float64
    IsNoise              bool
    CurrentState         *models.PatientClusterState
    OverrideEvents       []models.OverrideEvent
    DataModalityChange   bool
    DataModalityDetail   string
    DominantDomainDriver string
    EngagementStatus     string
}


type StabilityEngine struct {
    config *StabilityConfig
}


func NewStabilityEngine(config *StabilityConfig) *StabilityEngine {
    return &StabilityEngine{config: config}
}


func (e *StabilityEngine) Evaluate(input StabilityInput) models.StabilityDecision {
    decision := models.StabilityDecision{
        PatientID:       input.PatientID,
        RawClusterLabel: input.RawCluster,
        DomainDriver:    input.DominantDomainDriver,
    }


    // Compute confidence from membership probability
    decision.Confidence = input.MembershipProb


    // Case 1: First assignment — always accept
    if input.CurrentState == nil {
        decision.Decision = "ACCEPT"
        decision.StableClusterLabel = input.RawCluster
        decision.TransitionType = "INITIAL"
        decision.Reason = "First cluster assignment"
        return decision
    }


    currentCluster := input.CurrentState.CurrentStableCluster


    // Case 2: Same cluster — no transition needed
    if input.RawCluster == currentCluster {
        decision.Decision = "ACCEPT"
        decision.StableClusterLabel = currentCluster
        decision.Reason = "Same cluster as current stable assignment"
        return decision
    }


    // Case 3: Noise label — hold previous
    if input.IsNoise {
        decision.Decision = "HOLD_DWELL"
        decision.StableClusterLabel = currentCluster
        decision.Reason = "HDBSCAN assigned noise label (-1) — holding previous cluster"
        return decision
    }


    // Case 4: Data modality change — grace period
    if input.DataModalityChange {
        graceWeeks := e.config.CGMStartGraceWeeks
        if input.DataModalityDetail == "CGM_CESSATION" {
            graceWeeks = e.config.CGMStopGraceWeeks
        }
        decision.Decision = "HOLD_DWELL"
        decision.StableClusterLabel = currentCluster
        decision.Reason = fmt.Sprintf("Data modality change (%s) — %d-week grace period before reclassification",
            input.DataModalityDetail, graceWeeks)
        return decision
    }


    // Case 5: Check for override events — can bypass dwell and flap dampening
    if len(input.OverrideEvents) > 0 {
        for _, event := range input.OverrideEvents {
            if isQualifyingOverride(event) {
                decision.Decision = "OVERRIDE_EVENT"
                decision.StableClusterLabel = input.RawCluster
                decision.TransitionType = "GENUINE_TRANSITION"
                decision.TriggerEvent = event.EventType
                decision.Reason = fmt.Sprintf("Override event: %s — dwell policy bypassed", event.EventType)
                return decision
            }
        }
    }


    // Case 6: Flap detection — if flapping between same pair, hold at conservative
    if input.CurrentState.IsFlapping {
        for _, fp := range input.CurrentState.FlapPair {
            if fp == input.RawCluster {
                conservativeCluster := e.moreConservativeCluster(currentCluster, input.RawCluster)
                decision.Decision = "HOLD_FLAP"
                decision.StableClusterLabel = conservativeCluster
                decision.Reason = fmt.Sprintf("Flap detected: oscillating between %s and %s (%d times in 90 days) — "+
                    "held at more conservative cluster %s",
                    input.CurrentState.FlapPair[0], input.CurrentState.FlapPair[1],
                    input.CurrentState.FlapCount, conservativeCluster)
                return decision
            }
        }
    }


    // Case 7: Dwell policy — check if minimum dwell period has elapsed
    minDwell := e.config.MinDwellWeeks
    if input.CurrentState.FlapCount > 0 {
        minDwell = e.config.ExtendedDwellWeeks // extended dwell for previously flapping patients
    }
    minDwellDays := minDwell * 7


    if input.CurrentState.DwellDays < minDwellDays {
        decision.Decision = "HOLD_DWELL"
        decision.StableClusterLabel = currentCluster
        decision.Reason = fmt.Sprintf("Dwell policy: %d days in current cluster, minimum %d days required",
            input.CurrentState.DwellDays, minDwellDays)
        return decision
    }


    // Case 8: Past dwell, no flap, no override — accept transition
    decision.Decision = "ACCEPT"
    decision.StableClusterLabel = input.RawCluster
    decision.TransitionType = "GENUINE_TRANSITION"
    decision.Reason = fmt.Sprintf("Dwell period elapsed (%d days ≥ %d minimum), new cluster accepted",
        input.CurrentState.DwellDays, minDwellDays)
    return decision
}


func isQualifyingOverride(event models.OverrideEvent) bool {
    qualifiers := map[string]bool{
        "MHRI_CATEGORY_CROSSING":    true,
        "CKM_STAGE_TRANSITION":      true,
        "MEDICATION_CLASS_ADD":       true,
        "MEDICATION_CLASS_REMOVE":    true,
        "HOSPITALIZATION":           true,
        "CONCORDANT_DETERIORATION":  true,
        "CGM_INITIATION":           true,
        "CGM_CESSATION":            true,
    }
    return qualifiers[event.EventType]
}


func (e *StabilityEngine) moreConservativeCluster(a, b string) string {
    rankA, okA := e.config.ConservatismRank[a]
    rankB, okB := e.config.ConservatismRank[b]
    if !okA && !okB {
        return a // default to current
    }
    if !okA {
        return b
    }
    if !okB {
        return a
    }
    if rankA <= rankB {
        return a // lower rank = more conservative
    }
    return b
}
```

```go
// kb-20-patient-profile/internal/services/cluster_transition_classifier.go
package services


import (
    "fmt"
    "strings"
    "time"


    "kb-patient-profile/internal/models"
)


type TransitionContext struct {
    PreviousCluster      string
    NewCluster           string
    ConfidenceInNew      float64
    OverrideEvents       []models.OverrideEvent
    History              []models.ClusterTransitionRecord
    DominantDomainDriver string
    EngagementStatus     string
    DataModalityChange   bool
}


type TransitionClassification struct {
    Classification string `json:"classification"` // GENUINE_TRANSITION, PROBABLE_FLAP, UNCERTAIN, DWELL_HELD
    Narrative      string `json:"narrative"`       // human-readable explanation
    Confidence     float64 `json:"confidence"`
    IsClinicallyMeaningful bool `json:"clinically_meaningful"`
}


func ClassifyTransition(ctx TransitionContext) TransitionClassification {
    result := TransitionClassification{
        Confidence: ctx.ConfidenceInNew,
    }


    // Check for genuine transition indicators
    hasOverrideEvent := len(ctx.OverrideEvents) > 0
    highConfidence := ctx.ConfidenceInNew >= 0.5
    isReturn := isReturnToRecent(ctx.NewCluster, ctx.History)


    // Check for flap indicators
    isSamePairOscillation := detectSamePairOscillation(ctx.PreviousCluster, ctx.NewCluster, ctx.History)
    lowConfidence := ctx.ConfidenceInNew < 0.5


    switch {
    case hasOverrideEvent && highConfidence:
        result.Classification = "GENUINE_TRANSITION"
        result.IsClinicallyMeaningful = true
        result.Narrative = buildGenuineNarrative(ctx)


    case isSamePairOscillation && !hasOverrideEvent && lowConfidence:
        result.Classification = "PROBABLE_FLAP"
        result.IsClinicallyMeaningful = false
        result.Narrative = fmt.Sprintf("Oscillation between %s and %s detected without clinical event — "+
            "likely boundary instability rather than genuine phenotype change.",
            ctx.PreviousCluster, ctx.NewCluster)


    case hasOverrideEvent && !highConfidence:
        result.Classification = "GENUINE_TRANSITION"
        result.IsClinicallyMeaningful = true
        result.Narrative = buildGenuineNarrative(ctx) + " (note: moderate confidence in new cluster — monitor closely)"


    case !hasOverrideEvent && highConfidence && !isReturn:
        result.Classification = "UNCERTAIN"
        result.IsClinicallyMeaningful = false
        result.Narrative = fmt.Sprintf("Cluster change from %s to %s with reasonable confidence (%.0f%%) "+
            "but no clear clinical trigger. May reflect gradual phenotype drift.",
            ctx.PreviousCluster, ctx.NewCluster, ctx.ConfidenceInNew*100)


    default:
        result.Classification = "UNCERTAIN"
        result.IsClinicallyMeaningful = false
        result.Narrative = fmt.Sprintf("Cluster change from %s to %s — insufficient context to classify.",
            ctx.PreviousCluster, ctx.NewCluster)
    }


    return result
}


func buildGenuineNarrative(ctx TransitionContext) string {
    parts := []string{
        fmt.Sprintf("Phenotype transition %s → %s", ctx.PreviousCluster, ctx.NewCluster),
    }


    // Add trigger event context
    for _, event := range ctx.OverrideEvents {
        detail := event.Detail
        if detail == "" {
            detail = event.EventType
        }
        parts = append(parts, fmt.Sprintf("triggered by %s", detail))
    }


    // Add domain driver context
    if ctx.DominantDomainDriver != "" {
        parts = append(parts, fmt.Sprintf("driven primarily by %s domain change", ctx.DominantDomainDriver))
    }


    // Add therapy response narrative if medication event + improvement
    for _, event := range ctx.OverrideEvents {
        if event.EventType == "MEDICATION_CLASS_ADD" && ctx.DominantDomainDriver != "" {
            parts = append(parts, "— consistent with therapy response")
            break
        }
    }


    return strings.Join(parts, " ")
}


func isReturnToRecent(newCluster string, history []models.ClusterTransitionRecord) bool {
    lookback := time.Now().Add(-90 * 24 * time.Hour)
    for _, h := range history {
        if h.TransitionDate.After(lookback) && h.PreviousCluster == newCluster {
            return true
        }
    }
    return false
}


func detectSamePairOscillation(prev, curr string, history []models.ClusterTransitionRecord) bool {
    if len(history) < 1 {
        return false
    }
    lookback := time.Now().Add(-90 * 24 * time.Hour)
    for _, h := range history {
        if h.TransitionDate.After(lookback) {
            if (h.PreviousCluster == curr && h.NewCluster == prev) ||
                (h.PreviousCluster == prev && h.NewCluster == curr) {
                return true
            }
        }
    }
    return false
}
```

```python
# phenotype-clustering/src/stability_scorer.py
"""
Extracts stability-relevant metrics from HDBSCAN clustering results.
Feeds into the Go stability engine via the KB-20 updater.
"""
import numpy as np
from typing import List, Tuple, Optional




class StabilityScorer:
    """Computes per-patient stability metrics from HDBSCAN output."""


    def __init__(self, labels: np.ndarray, probabilities: np.ndarray,
                 embedding: np.ndarray, centroids: np.ndarray):
        self.labels = labels
        self.probabilities = probabilities
        self.embedding = embedding
        self.centroids = centroids


    def compute_patient_stability(self, patient_idx: int) -> dict:
        """Compute stability metrics for a single patient."""
        label = int(self.labels[patient_idx])
        prob = float(self.probabilities[patient_idx]) if self.probabilities is not None else 0.0


        if label == -1:
            return {
                "cluster_label": "NOISE",
                "membership_probability": 0.0,
                "distance_to_centroid": float("inf"),
                "distance_to_nearest_other": 0.0,
                "separability_ratio": 0.0,
                "is_noise": True,
                "confidence": "NONE",
            }


        patient_embedding = self.embedding[patient_idx]


        # Distance to assigned centroid
        dist_own = np.linalg.norm(patient_embedding - self.centroids[label])


        # Distance to nearest OTHER centroid
        dist_nearest = float("inf")
        for i, centroid in enumerate(self.centroids):
            if i == label:
                continue
            d = np.linalg.norm(patient_embedding - centroid)
            if d < dist_nearest:
                dist_nearest = d


        separability = dist_nearest / max(dist_own, 1e-10)


        # Confidence classification
        if prob >= 0.7 and separability >= 2.0:
            confidence = "HIGH"
        elif prob >= 0.4:
            confidence = "MODERATE"
        else:
            confidence = "LOW"


        return {
            "cluster_label": f"CLUSTER_{label}",
            "membership_probability": round(prob, 4),
            "distance_to_centroid": round(float(dist_own), 4),
            "distance_to_nearest_other": round(float(dist_nearest), 4),
            "separability_ratio": round(float(separability), 4),
            "is_noise": False,
            "confidence": confidence,
        }


    def compute_all_stability(self) -> List[dict]:
        """Compute stability metrics for all patients."""
        return [self.compute_patient_stability(i) for i in range(len(self.labels))]


    def get_boundary_patients(self, prob_threshold: float = 0.4) -> List[int]:
        """Returns indices of patients near cluster boundaries (low probability)."""
        boundary = []
        for i, (label, prob) in enumerate(zip(self.labels, self.probabilities)):
            if label != -1 and prob < prob_threshold:
                boundary.append(i)
        return boundary
```

```go
// kb-23-decision-cards/internal/services/phenotype_transition_cards.go
package services


import (
    "fmt"


    "kb-patient-profile/internal/models"
)


type PhenotypeCard struct {
    CardType  string `json:"card_type"`
    Urgency   string `json:"urgency"`
    Title     string `json:"title"`
    Rationale string `json:"rationale"`
    Actions   []string `json:"actions"`
}


func EvaluatePhenotypeTransitionCards(
    decision models.StabilityDecision,
    classification TransitionClassification,
) []PhenotypeCard {
    var cards []PhenotypeCard


    switch decision.Decision {
    case "OVERRIDE_EVENT":
        if classification.IsClinicallyMeaningful {
            cards = append(cards, PhenotypeCard{
                CardType: "PHENOTYPE_TRANSITION",
                Urgency:  "ROUTINE",
                Title: fmt.Sprintf("Phenotype Transition: %s → %s",
                    decision.RawClusterLabel, decision.StableClusterLabel),
                Rationale: classification.Narrative,
                Actions: []string{
                    "Review therapy pathway for new phenotype",
                    "Update similar-patient comparisons based on new cluster",
                },
            })
        }


    case "HOLD_FLAP":
        cards = append(cards, PhenotypeCard{
            CardType: "PHENOTYPE_INSTABILITY",
            Urgency:  "ROUTINE",
            Title:    "Phenotype Classification Unstable — Cluster Boundary Patient",
            Rationale: fmt.Sprintf("%s. Patient's clinical features place them near the boundary "+
                "between phenotype clusters. Current therapy pathway held at more conservative option "+
                "pending stabilization.", decision.Reason),
            Actions: []string{
                "Review which clinical domain is driving instability",
                "Stabilize the oscillating domain before reassessing phenotype",
                "Consider manual phenotype assignment if oscillation persists >3 months",
            },
        })


    case "HOLD_DWELL":
        // Usually no card — dwell holds are silent. Exception: data modality change.
        if decision.Reason != "" && contains(decision.Reason, "modality") {
            cards = append(cards, PhenotypeCard{
                CardType: "DATA_MODALITY_CHANGE",
                Urgency:  "ROUTINE",
                Title:    "CGM Data Change — Phenotype Reassessment Pending",
                Rationale: "Patient's data source changed (CGM started or stopped). Phenotype cluster " +
                    "held at current assignment during grace period to avoid data-driven reclassification.",
                Actions: []string{
                    "Allow 2-4 weeks of new data modality before reassessing",
                    "Current therapy pathway unchanged",
                },
            })
        }
    }


    return cards
}


func contains(s, substr string) bool {
    return len(s) >= len(substr) && (s == substr || len(s) > 0 && searchSubstring(s, substr))
}


func searchSubstring(s, sub string) bool {
    for i := 0; i <= len(s)-len(sub); i++ {
        if s[i:i+len(sub)] == sub {
            return true
        }
    }
    return false
}
```

**Clinical evidence base:**
- Von Luxburg et al. 2010 (Statistics and Computing) — clustering stability as quality criterion
- Ahlqvist et al. 2018 (Lancet D&E) — 15-20% boundary reclassification in diabetes subtyping
- Hennig 2007 (Journal of Classification) — dissolution point concept for cluster stability
- Dagliati et al. 2018 (J Biomed Inform) — temporal trajectory stability predicts outcomes


**The cross-domain innovation in stability:** This isn't just "prevent flapping." It's a system that understands *why* a patient's phenotype changed — was it a medication response (genuine, celebrate), a measurement behavior change (data artifact, ignore), a seasonal variation (temporary, wait), or boundary noise (dampened, monitor). That classification requires simultaneously holding the patient's clustering output, their IOR intervention timeline, their engagement phenotype, their domain decomposition, and their data modality context. No clustering pipeline in the literature does this. They all produce labels and walk away. This system produces labels, evaluates them against clinical context, and decides whether to trust them.
