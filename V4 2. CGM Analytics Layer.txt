# CGM Analytics Layer — Implementation Plan


## Clinical Rationale


**Why this is critical gap #1 for clinical value:**


The platform currently captures CGM readings as point-in-time glucose values (`DEVICE_READING` with `readingType: "GLUCOSE"`) and routes them through Module 1b into the canonical pipeline. But a single CGM reading of 198 mg/dL is clinically meaningless without temporal context. Was this a transient post-meal spike that returned to 120 within 90 minutes (benign, normal physiology) or part of a sustained 6-hour plateau above 180 (pathological, indicating insulin resistance or medication failure)? The answer determines whether the clinician acts or waits. Without CGM analytics, the platform treats continuous glucose data the same way it treats a single fingerstick — discarding 95% of the clinical signal.


**The International Consensus on CGM Metrics (Battelino et al. 2023, updated from 2019)** established 14 standardized metrics that every CGM analytics system must compute. These aren't optional academic exercises — RSSDI 2023, ADS-ADEA 2024, ADA Standards of Care 2025, and NICE NG28 (2024 update) all reference these metrics as primary glycaemic management endpoints alongside HbA1c. The consensus explicitly states that TIR (Time in Range) should be a primary clinical target, with >70% time in 70-180 mg/dL for most adults with T2DM.


**The specific metrics and their clinical significance:**


**Time in Range (TIR, 70-180 mg/dL):** The single most important CGM metric. Each 5% increase in TIR corresponds to approximately 0.5% decrease in HbA1c (Beck et al., 2019). Target: >70% for most T2DM adults, >50% for frail/elderly (relaxed per RSSDI 2023 and RACGP 2024). A 1% increase in TIR is associated with 5% reduction in retinopathy progression risk (DCCT reanalysis, Lu et al. 2021). This is the metric that replaces HbA1c as the primary glycaemic target for CGM-wearing patients.


**Time Below Range (TBR):** Level 1 hypoglycaemia (<70 mg/dL) — target <4% of time. Level 2 severe hypoglycaemia (<54 mg/dL) — target <1%. In diabetic patients on metformin + sulfonylurea (common Indian regimen), TBR is the safety signal that triggers medication adjustment before a severe hypoglycaemic event occurs. For patients on insulin, TBR <54 directly correlates with severe hypoglycaemia event risk (Heinemann et al., 2023).


**Time Above Range (TAR):** Level 1 hyperglycaemia (>180 mg/dL) — target <25%. Level 2 severe hyperglycaemia (>250 mg/dL) — target <5%. In Rajesh Kumar's scenario (HbA1c 8.2, CGM reading 198), sustained TAR >250 would indicate need for therapy intensification even if HbA1c hasn't been rechecked. TAR >250 for >2 hours correlates with increased oxidative stress and endothelial dysfunction (Ceriello et al., 2020).


**Glucose Management Indicator (GMI):** Estimated HbA1c from CGM data. GMI = 3.31 + (0.02392 × mean glucose in mg/dL). Clinically useful because it updates every 14 days from CGM data rather than waiting 3 months for lab HbA1c. When GMI and lab HbA1c diverge by >0.5%, it indicates either hemoglobin variant, anemia, or lab assay interference — a diagnostic finding in itself (Bergenstal et al., 2018).


**Coefficient of Variation (CV):** SD/mean × 100. CV ≤36% indicates stable glucose; CV >36% indicates unstable/labile diabetes. This threshold was validated by Monnier et al. (2017) and adopted by the International Consensus. CV >36% is an independent predictor of hypoglycaemia risk regardless of mean glucose. For patients with high CV, the therapeutic priority shifts from lowering mean glucose to reducing variability — a fundamentally different strategy (typically adding or adjusting basal insulin rather than intensifying bolus/prandial therapy).


**Mean Glucose and Standard Deviation:** Foundation metrics. Mean glucose maps to GMI. SD captures absolute variability. Together they form the glycaemic "fingerprint."


**Ambulatory Glucose Profile (AGP):** Visual representation showing median, IQR, and 10th/90th percentiles across a 24-hour period, aggregated over 14 days. The AGP reveals temporal patterns invisible in summary statistics: dawn phenomenon (pre-breakfast spike due to hepatic glucose output), post-prandial peak timing, nocturnal hypoglycaemia, and medication-effect timing. Computing AGP percentiles at 15-minute or 30-minute resolution across 24 hours requires bucketing readings by time-of-day.


**Glycaemic Risk Index (GRI):** Introduced by Klonoff et al. (2023), adopted in ADA 2025. A single composite metric that integrates hypoglycaemia risk and hyperglycaemia risk into a 0-100 scale. GRI = (3.0 × VLow) + (2.4 × Low) + (1.6 × VHigh) + (0.8 × High), where VLow = % time <54, Low = % time 54-70, VHigh = % time >250, High = % time 180-250. GRI zones: A (best, GRI ≤20), B (GRI 21-40), C (GRI 41-60), D (GRI 61-80), E (worst, GRI >80). This is the metric that most directly maps to clinical action urgency.

**India-Specific CGM Considerations:**


CGM adoption in India is accelerating but unevenly distributed. The Abbott FreeStyle Libre is the dominant device (~85% market share), with the Libre 2 providing 14-day sensor life and 1-minute glucose readings (but the reader/app captures at 15-minute intervals for most users). Dexcom G7 has entered the Indian market but at significantly higher cost. Key India-specific factors:


- **Intermittent scanning vs. real-time:** Most Indian Libre users do intermittent scanning (isCGM), not real-time CGM (rtCGM). This means data gaps when the patient doesn't scan for >8 hours (the sensor stores 8 hours of data; older data is lost). The analytics engine must handle data gaps gracefully and compute metrics only when sufficient data exists (≥70% of readings present in a 24-hour period per International Consensus).
- **14-day sensor cycles:** Indian CGM data arrives in 14-day bursts aligned with sensor life. The analytics engine should compute standard 14-day AGP reports at sensor change, not arbitrary rolling windows.
- **Ramadan fasting:** During Ramadan, Muslim diabetic patients (15% of India market) fast from dawn to sunset. CGM data during Ramadan shows dramatically altered patterns — extended fasting euglycaemia/hypoglycaemia followed by post-Iftar hyperglycaemia spikes. The analytics engine must detect Ramadan-period data and adjust TIR interpretations (IDF-DAR 2021 practical guidelines recommend relaxed TBR targets and modified meal-glucose pairing during Ramadan).
- **Cost-driven intermittent use:** Many Indian patients use CGM for 2-4 weeks, then revert to SMBG for cost reasons. The platform must handle intermittent CGM data alongside SMBG data, using CGM analytics when available and gracefully falling back to SMBG-based scoring.


**Australia-Specific CGM Considerations:**


- **NDSS subsidy:** The National Diabetes Services Scheme subsidizes CGM for T1DM and some T2DM patients on insulin. CGM data from subsidized devices often flows through the My Health Record system — the FHIR outbound gap (#9) becomes directly relevant here.
- **Indigenous populations:** Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander communities in remote areas increasingly use CGM through NACCHO-managed health services. Data connectivity is intermittent. The analytics engine must compute metrics from locally stored data that syncs when connectivity is available — potentially receiving 14 days of data in a single burst after an offline period.
- **ADS-ADEA position statement 2024:** Explicitly recommends TIR as co-primary endpoint with HbA1c for all CGM-wearing patients. RACGP 2024 guidelines endorse the same position.

## Architecture Overview


The CGM analytics system spans four components:


1. **Module 3 (Flink — Comprehensive CDS):** Extended with a CGM analytics operator that computes streaming TIR/TBR/TAR/CV/mean/SD from the CGM reading stream. Emits `flink.cgm-analytics` events at configurable intervals (every reading + daily summary + 14-day report). This is where real-time alerting happens (nocturnal hypoglycaemia detection, sustained hyperglycaemia).


2. **KB-26 (Metabolic Digital Twin):** Receives CGM analytics summaries and integrates them into the MHRI glucose domain scoring. When CGM data is available, MHRI glucose domain switches from FBG/PPBG snapshot scoring to CGM-derived TIR/CV/GMI scoring — fundamentally more accurate. Computes AGP percentile profiles for 14-day reports. Stores CGM metric history for trajectory analysis.


3. **KB-20 (Patient Profile):** Stores patient's CGM device type, sensor status, data availability windows. Exposes CGM status endpoint for card generation context (does this patient have CGM data? how recent? how complete?).


4. **KB-23 (Decision Cards):** Consumes CGM analytics to generate CGM-specific decision cards. TBR >4% triggers hypoglycaemia risk card. TAR >250 sustained >2 hours triggers hyperglycaemia urgency card. CV >36% triggers variability-focused therapy review card. GMI-HbA1c discrepancy >0.5% triggers investigation card.


**Data flow:**
```
CGM Device → Patient App → Ingestion Service → Module 1b (canonical)
                                                      ↓
                                              Module 3 CGM Operator
                                              (streaming TIR/TBR/TAR/CV)
                                                      ↓
                                              flink.cgm-analytics topic
                                                ↓              ↓
                                            KB-26            KB-23
                                    (MHRI glucose domain)  (CGM cards)
                                              ↓
                                    KB-20 (v4-state PATCH)
```


**Critical design decisions:**


1. **Minimum data requirement:** Per International Consensus, CGM metrics are only valid when ≥70% of readings are present over the computation period. For a 14-day period with readings every 15 minutes (96/day × 14 = 1,344 expected), minimum is 941 readings. The system must track data sufficiency and flag metrics as `LOW_CONFIDENCE` when below 70%.


2. **Sensor warm-up exclusion:** First 60 minutes after sensor insertion produce unreliable readings. The system must detect sensor-start events and exclude warm-up readings from analytics.


3. **Calibration detection:** Some CGM systems require fingerstick calibrations. Calibration readings should be flagged and excluded from CGM analytics (they're SMBG values, not interstitial glucose).


4. **Compression artifact handling:** CGM sensors have accuracy limitations at extremes (typically ±15 mg/dL accuracy in 70-180 range, wider at extremes). The system should not over-interpret readings near range boundaries. A reading of 68 mg/dL might or might not be true hypoglycaemia. This is handled by the International Consensus recommendation to use sustained below-range (≥15 consecutive minutes) rather than single-reading thresholds.

## Phase G2: Flink CGM Analytics Operator


### Step 4: Create CGM data models for Flink


```java
// flink-processing/.../models/CGMReadingBuffer.java
package com.cardiofit.flink.models;


import java.io.Serializable;
import java.util.*;


/**
 * Ring buffer for CGM readings in Flink keyed state.
 * Stores up to 14 days of readings (96/day × 14 = 1,344 max).
 * Uses HashMap instead of EnumMap to avoid Kryo serialization issues.
 */
public class CGMReadingBuffer implements Serializable {
    private static final long serialVersionUID = 1L;
    private static final int MAX_READINGS = 1_500; // slightly above 14-day capacity


    private final List<Long> timestamps;     // epoch millis
    private final List<Double> glucoseValues; // mg/dL
    private Long sensorStartTime;            // null if unknown
    private int totalReadingsReceived;
    private int readingsExcluded;            // warm-up, calibration, etc.


    public CGMReadingBuffer() {
        this.timestamps = new ArrayList<>(MAX_READINGS);
        this.glucoseValues = new ArrayList<>(MAX_READINGS);
        this.totalReadingsReceived = 0;
        this.readingsExcluded = 0;
    }


    /**
     * Add a CGM reading. Returns false if excluded (warm-up, duplicate, etc.)
     */
    public boolean addReading(long timestampMs, double glucoseMgDl) {
        totalReadingsReceived++;


        // Exclude sensor warm-up (first 60 minutes)
        if (sensorStartTime != null && timestampMs - sensorStartTime < 60 * 60 * 1000L) {
            readingsExcluded++;
            return false;
        }


        // Exclude physiologically impossible values
        if (glucoseMgDl < 20.0 || glucoseMgDl > 500.0) {
            readingsExcluded++;
            return false;
        }


        // Maintain sorted order by timestamp
        int insertIdx = Collections.binarySearch(timestamps, timestampMs);
        if (insertIdx >= 0) {
            // Duplicate timestamp — skip
            readingsExcluded++;
            return false;
        }
        insertIdx = -(insertIdx + 1);
        timestamps.add(insertIdx, timestampMs);
        glucoseValues.add(insertIdx, glucoseMgDl);


        // Evict oldest if over capacity
        while (timestamps.size() > MAX_READINGS) {
            timestamps.remove(0);
            glucoseValues.remove(0);
        }


        return true;
    }


    public void setSensorStartTime(long sensorStartMs) {
        this.sensorStartTime = sensorStartMs;
    }


    /**
     * Returns readings within the specified window.
     * @param windowStartMs inclusive start
     * @param windowEndMs inclusive end
     */
    public List<Double> getReadingsInWindow(long windowStartMs, long windowEndMs) {
        List<Double> result = new ArrayList<>();
        for (int i = 0; i < timestamps.size(); i++) {
            long ts = timestamps.get(i);
            if (ts >= windowStartMs && ts <= windowEndMs) {
                result.add(glucoseValues.get(i));
            }
        }
        return result;
    }


    /**
     * Returns timestamped readings in window for AGP bucketing.
     */
    public List<long[]> getTimestampedReadingsInWindow(long windowStartMs, long windowEndMs) {
        List<long[]> result = new ArrayList<>();
        for (int i = 0; i < timestamps.size(); i++) {
            long ts = timestamps.get(i);
            if (ts >= windowStartMs && ts <= windowEndMs) {
                result.add(new long[]{ts, Double.doubleToLongBits(glucoseValues.get(i))});
            }
        }
        return result;
    }


    /**
     * Computes data sufficiency as percentage of expected readings present.
     * @param windowDays number of days in window
     * @param expectedPerDay expected readings per day (default 96 for 15-min intervals)
     */
    public double computeCoverage(long windowStartMs, long windowEndMs, int expectedPerDay) {
        int expected = (int) ((windowEndMs - windowStartMs) / (86400_000L)) * expectedPerDay;
        if (expected <= 0) return 0.0;
        int actual = 0;
        for (long ts : timestamps) {
            if (ts >= windowStartMs && ts <= windowEndMs) actual++;
        }
        return (double) actual / expected;
    }


    public int size() { return timestamps.size(); }
    public int getTotalReadingsReceived() { return totalReadingsReceived; }
    public int getReadingsExcluded() { return readingsExcluded; }


    /**
     * Returns consecutive readings below threshold, with timestamps.
     * Used for sustained hypo/hyper detection.
     */
    public List<ConsecutiveRun> findConsecutiveRunsBelowThreshold(double threshold, long windowStartMs, long windowEndMs) {
        List<ConsecutiveRun> runs = new ArrayList<>();
        Long runStart = null;
        int runCount = 0;


        for (int i = 0; i < timestamps.size(); i++) {
            long ts = timestamps.get(i);
            if (ts < windowStartMs || ts > windowEndMs) continue;


            if (glucoseValues.get(i) < threshold) {
                if (runStart == null) {
                    runStart = ts;
                    runCount = 1;
                } else {
                    runCount++;
                }
            } else {
                if (runStart != null) {
                    runs.add(new ConsecutiveRun(runStart, ts, runCount));
                    runStart = null;
                    runCount = 0;
                }
            }
        }
        // Close final run if still open
        if (runStart != null && !timestamps.isEmpty()) {
            runs.add(new ConsecutiveRun(runStart, timestamps.get(timestamps.size() - 1), runCount));
        }
        return runs;
    }


    public List<ConsecutiveRun> findConsecutiveRunsAboveThreshold(double threshold, long windowStartMs, long windowEndMs) {
        List<ConsecutiveRun> runs = new ArrayList<>();
        Long runStart = null;
        int runCount = 0;


        for (int i = 0; i < timestamps.size(); i++) {
            long ts = timestamps.get(i);
            if (ts < windowStartMs || ts > windowEndMs) continue;


            if (glucoseValues.get(i) > threshold) {
                if (runStart == null) {
                    runStart = ts;
                    runCount = 1;
                } else {
                    runCount++;
                }
            } else {
                if (runStart != null) {
                    runs.add(new ConsecutiveRun(runStart, ts, runCount));
                    runStart = null;
                    runCount = 0;
                }
            }
        }
        if (runStart != null && !timestamps.isEmpty()) {
            runs.add(new ConsecutiveRun(runStart, timestamps.get(timestamps.size() - 1), runCount));
        }
        return runs;
    }


    public static class ConsecutiveRun implements Serializable {
        public final long startMs;
        public final long endMs;
        public final int readingCount;


        public ConsecutiveRun(long startMs, long endMs, int readingCount) {
```

```java
// flink-processing/.../operators/Module3_CGMAnalytics.java
package com.cardiofit.flink.operators;


import com.cardiofit.flink.models.CGMAnalyticsEvent;
import com.cardiofit.flink.models.CGMReadingBuffer;


import java.util.*;


/**
 * CGM Analytics engine implementing International Consensus on CGM Metrics
 * (Battelino et al. 2023), GRI (Klonoff et al. 2023).
 *
 * Computes: TIR, TBR (L1/L2), TAR (L1/L2), CV, GMI, GRI, AGP percentiles.
 * Handles data sufficiency validation per consensus (≥70% coverage required).
 */
public class Module3_CGMAnalytics {


    // Range thresholds (mg/dL) — International Consensus 2023
    private static final double VERY_LOW = 54.0;
    private static final double LOW = 70.0;
    private static final double HIGH = 180.0;
    private static final double VERY_HIGH = 250.0;


    // GMI formula constants — Bergenstal et al. 2018
    private static final double GMI_INTERCEPT = 3.31;
    private static final double GMI_SLOPE = 0.02392;


    // GRI weights — Klonoff et al. 2023
    private static final double GRI_W_VLOW = 3.0;
    private static final double GRI_W_LOW = 2.4;
    private static final double GRI_W_VHIGH = 1.6;
    private static final double GRI_W_HIGH = 0.8;


    // CV stability threshold — Monnier et al. 2017
    private static final double CV_STABLE_THRESHOLD = 36.0;


    // Data sufficiency
    private static final int READINGS_PER_DAY = 96;
    private static final double MIN_COVERAGE_PCT = 70.0;


    /**
     * Compute all CGM metrics from a list of glucose values.
     * @param readings glucose values in mg/dL
     * @param windowDays number of days the readings span
     */
    public CGMAnalyticsEvent computeMetrics(List<Double> readings, int windowDays) {
        int n = readings.size();
        int expected = windowDays * READINGS_PER_DAY;
        double coverage = expected > 0 ? (double) n / expected * 100.0 : 0.0;
        boolean sufficient = coverage >= MIN_COVERAGE_PCT;


        String confidence;
        if (coverage >= 70.0) confidence = "HIGH";
        else if (coverage >= 50.0) confidence = "MODERATE";
        else confidence = "LOW";


        // Range classification
        int countVeryLow = 0, countLow = 0, countInRange = 0, countHigh = 0, countVeryHigh = 0;
        double sum = 0;


        for (double g : readings) {
            sum += g;
            if (g < VERY_LOW) countVeryLow++;
            else if (g < LOW) countLow++;
            else if (g <= HIGH) countInRange++;
            else if (g <= VERY_HIGH) countHigh++;
            else countVeryHigh++;
        }


        double mean = n > 0 ? sum / n : 0;


        // Standard deviation
        double sumSqDiff = 0;
        for (double g : readings) {
            sumSqDiff += (g - mean) * (g - mean);
        }
        double sd = n > 1 ? Math.sqrt(sumSqDiff / (n - 1)) : 0;


        // CV
        double cv = mean > 0 ? (sd / mean) * 100.0 : 0;


        // Time in range percentages
        double tirPct = n > 0 ? (double) countInRange / n * 100.0 : 0;
        double tbrL1Pct = n > 0 ? (double) countLow / n * 100.0 : 0;
        double tbrL2Pct = n > 0 ? (double) countVeryLow / n * 100.0 : 0;
        double tarL1Pct = n > 0 ? (double) countHigh / n * 100.0 : 0;
        double tarL2Pct = n > 0 ? (double) countVeryHigh / n * 100.0 : 0;


        // GMI
        double gmi = GMI_INTERCEPT + (GMI_SLOPE * mean);


        // GRI
        double gri = (GRI_W_VLOW * tbrL2Pct) + (GRI_W_LOW * tbrL1Pct)
                    + (GRI_W_VHIGH * tarL2Pct) + (GRI_W_HIGH * tarL1Pct);
        gri = Math.min(gri, 100.0); // cap at 100


        return CGMAnalyticsEvent.builder()
                .coveragePct(Math.round(coverage * 100.0) / 100.0)
                .totalReadings(n)
                .windowDays(windowDays)
                .sufficientData(sufficient)
                .confidenceLevel(confidence)
                .meanGlucose(Math.round(mean * 10.0) / 10.0)
                .sdGlucose(Math.round(sd * 10.0) / 10.0)
                .cvPct(Math.round(cv * 10.0) / 10.0)
                .tirPct(Math.round(tirPct * 100.0) / 100.0)
                .tbrL1Pct(Math.round(tbrL1Pct * 100.0) / 100.0)
                .tbrL2Pct(Math.round(tbrL2Pct * 100.0) / 100.0)
                .tarL1Pct(Math.round(tarL1Pct * 100.0) / 100.0)
                .tarL2Pct(Math.round(tarL2Pct * 100.0) / 100.0)
                .gmi(Math.round(gmi * 100.0) / 100.0)
                .gri(Math.round(gri * 100.0) / 100.0)
                .build();
    }


    /**
     * Detect sustained hypoglycaemia (consecutive readings below threshold for >= durationMinutes).
     */
    public boolean detectSustainedHypo(CGMReadingBuffer buffer, long windowStart, long windowEnd,
                                        double threshold, int durationMinutes) {
        List<CGMReadingBuffer.ConsecutiveRun> runs =
            buffer.findConsecutiveRunsBelowThreshold(threshold, windowStart, windowEnd);
        for (CGMReadingBuffer.ConsecutiveRun run : runs) {
            if (run.durationMinutes() >= durationMinutes) {
                return true;
            }
        }
        return false;
    }


    /**
     * Detect sustained hyperglycaemia (consecutive readings above threshold for >= durationMinutes).
     */
    public boolean detectSustainedHyper(CGMReadingBuffer buffer, long windowStart, long windowEnd,
                                         double threshold, int durationMinutes) {
        List<CGMReadingBuffer.ConsecutiveRun> runs =
            buffer.findConsecutiveRunsAboveThreshold(threshold, windowStart, windowEnd);
        for (CGMReadingBuffer.ConsecutiveRun run : runs) {
            if (run.durationMinutes() >= durationMinutes) {
                return true;
            }
        }
        return false;
    }


    /**
     * Detect nocturnal hypoglycaemia (hypo during 00:00-06:00 window).
     */
    public boolean detectNocturnalHypo(CGMReadingBuffer buffer, long nocturnalStart, long nocturnalEnd,
                                        double threshold, int durationMinutes) {
        return detectSustainedHypo(buffer, nocturnalStart, nocturnalEnd, threshold, durationMinutes);
    }


    /**
     * Compute AGP percentile profile for a 24-hour period.
     * Buckets readings by time-of-day into 30-minute intervals (48 buckets).
     * Computes p10, p25, p50, p75, p90 for each bucket.
     *
     * @param timestampedReadings list of [timestampMs, glucoseAsLongBits]
     * @param minDaysPerBucket minimum days contributing to a bucket for it to be valid
     * @return map of bucket index (0-47) → [p10, p25, p50, p75, p90], null values for insufficient data
     */
    public Map<Integer, double[]> computeAGP(List<long[]> timestampedReadings, int minDaysPerBucket) {
        // Bucket readings by time-of-day
        Map<Integer, List<Double>> buckets = new HashMap<>();
        for (int i = 0; i < 48; i++) buckets.put(i, new ArrayList<>());


        for (long[] reading : timestampedReadings) {
            long ts = reading[0];
            double glucose = Double.longBitsToDouble(reading[1]);


            // Extract time-of-day in minutes from timestamp
            Calendar cal = Calendar.getInstance(TimeZone.getTimeZone("UTC"));
            cal.setTimeInMillis(ts);
            int minuteOfDay = cal.get(Calendar.HOUR_OF_DAY) * 60 + cal.get(Calendar.MINUTE);
            int bucket = minuteOfDay / 30;
            if (bucket >= 0 && bucket < 48) {
                buckets.get(bucket).add(glucose);
            }
        }


        // Compute percentiles for each bucket
        Map<Integer, double[]> agp = new HashMap<>();
        for (int i = 0; i < 48; i++) {
            List<Double> values = buckets.get(i);
            if (values.size() < minDaysPerBucket) {
                agp.put(i, null); // insufficient data for this bucket
                continue;
            }
            Collections.sort(values);
            agp.put(i, new double[]{
                percentile(values, 10),
                percentile(values, 25),
                percentile(values, 50),
                percentile(values, 75),
                percentile(values, 90)
            });
        }
        return agp;
    }


    private double percentile(List<Double> sorted, int p) {
        double idx = (p / 100.0) * (sorted.size() - 1);
        int lower = (int) Math.floor(idx);
        int upper = (int) Math.ceil(idx);
        if (lower == upper) return sorted.get(lower);
        double frac = idx - lower;
        return sorted.get(lower) * (1 - frac) + sorted.get(upper) * frac;
    }
}
```

```go
// kb-26-metabolic-digital-twin/internal/services/cgm_analytics.go
package services


import (
    "math"
    "time"


    "kb-metabolic-digital-twin/internal/models"
)


// ComputeGlucoseDomainScore computes the MHRI glucose domain (35% weight).
// When CGM data is available and sufficient, uses TIR/CV/GRI for scoring.
// When CGM data is unavailable or insufficient, falls back to FBG/HbA1c.
func ComputeGlucoseDomainScore(fbg *float64, hba1c *float64, cgm *models.CGMPeriodReport) float64 {
    // Priority: CGM with sufficient data > FBG/HbA1c snapshot
    if cgm != nil && cgm.SufficientData {
        return computeCGMBasedScore(cgm)
    }


    // Fallback to point-in-time scoring
    return computeFBGBasedScore(fbg, hba1c)
}


func computeCGMBasedScore(cgm *models.CGMPeriodReport) float64 {
    // CGM glucose domain score (0-100) composited from:
    // TIR (40% weight): 0-100 scaled, target >70%
    // CV stability (20% weight): bonus for CV ≤36%, penalty for CV >36%
    // GRI (25% weight): inverse scale, Zone A = 100, Zone E = 0
    // TBR safety (15% weight): heavy penalty for any time below range


    tirScore := scaleLinear(cgm.TIRPct, 0, 100, 0, 100) // direct mapping
    tirScore = math.Min(tirScore, 100)


    cvScore := 100.0
    if cgm.CVPct > 36.0 {
        cvScore = scaleLinear(cgm.CVPct, 36, 60, 70, 0) // 36% → 70, 60% → 0
        cvScore = math.Max(cvScore, 0)
    }


    griScore := scaleLinear(100-cgm.GRI, 0, 100, 0, 100) // inverse: GRI 0 → 100, GRI 100 → 0


    // TBR penalty: exponential penalty for time below range
    tbrTotal := cgm.TBRL1Pct + cgm.TBRL2Pct
    tbrScore := 100.0
    if tbrTotal > 0 {
        tbrScore = math.Max(0, 100-tbrTotal*15) // each 1% TBR costs 15 points
    }
    if cgm.TBRL2Pct > 1.0 {
        tbrScore *= 0.5 // halve score if severe hypo exceeds target
    }


    composite := (tirScore * 0.40) + (cvScore * 0.20) + (griScore * 0.25) + (tbrScore * 0.15)
    return math.Round(composite*10) / 10
}


func computeFBGBasedScore(fbg *float64, hba1c *float64) float64 {
    score := 50.0 // default mid-range when no data


    if hba1c != nil {
        // HbA1c scoring: 6.5% → 85, 7.0% → 70, 8.0% → 40, 9.0% → 20, 10+ → 5
        score = scaleLinear(*hba1c, 6.0, 10.0, 90, 5)
        score = math.Max(5, math.Min(95, score))
    }


    if fbg != nil {
        // FBG modifier: <100 → +10, 100-130 → 0, 130-180 → -10, >180 → -20
        fbgMod := 0.0
        if *fbg < 100 {
            fbgMod = 10.0
        } else if *fbg > 180 {
            fbgMod = -20.0
        } else if *fbg > 130 {
            fbgMod = -10.0
        }
        score += fbgMod
    }


    return math.Max(0, math.Min(100, score))
}


func scaleLinear(value, inMin, inMax, outMin, outMax float64) float64 {
    if inMax == inMin {
        return outMin
    }
    return outMin + (value-inMin)*(outMax-outMin)/(inMax-inMin)
}


// GMIDiscrepancy represents the difference between CGM-derived GMI and lab HbA1c.
type GMIDiscrepancy struct {
    GMI           float64 `json:"gmi"`
    LabHbA1c      float64 `json:"lab_hba1c"`
    Difference    float64 `json:"difference"`
    Discrepant    bool    `json:"discrepant"`
    PossibleCauses []string `json:"possible_causes,omitempty"`
}


// DetectGMIDiscrepancy checks if GMI and lab HbA1c diverge significantly.
func DetectGMIDiscrepancy(gmi, labHbA1c float64) GMIDiscrepancy {
    diff := math.Abs(gmi - labHbA1c)
    result := GMIDiscrepancy{
        GMI:        gmi,
        LabHbA1c:   labHbA1c,
        Difference: math.Round(diff*10) / 10,
        Discrepant: diff > 0.5,
    }


    if result.Discrepant {
        if gmi > labHbA1c+0.5 {
            result.PossibleCauses = []string{
                "Recent glycaemic improvement not yet reflected in HbA1c",
                "Hemoglobin variant affecting HbA1c assay",
                "Conditions reducing red cell lifespan (hemolytic anemia, recent blood loss)",
            }
        } else {
            result.PossibleCauses = []string{
                "Recent glycaemic deterioration not yet reflected in HbA1c",
                "Iron deficiency anemia (falsely elevates HbA1c)",
                "Hemoglobin variant affecting HbA1c assay",
            }
        }
    }
    return result
}


// CGMTrend represents the trajectory of CGM metrics over time.
type CGMTrend struct {
    TIRTrend       string  `json:"tir_trend"`        // IMPROVING, STABLE, DETERIORATING
    TIRSlopePerDay float64 `json:"tir_slope_per_day"` // % TIR change per day
    CVTrend        string  `json:"cv_trend"`
    CVSlopePerDay  float64 `json:"cv_slope_per_day"`
    DataPoints     int     `json:"data_points"`
}


// ComputeCGMTrend computes OLS trend over daily CGM summaries.
func ComputeCGMTrend(summaries []models.CGMDailySummary) CGMTrend {
    if len(summaries) < 3 {
        return CGMTrend{TIRTrend: "INSUFFICIENT_DATA", CVTrend: "INSUFFICIENT_DATA"}
    }


    baseTime := summaries[0].Date
    n := float64(len(summaries))
    var sumX, sumTIR, sumXTIR, sumX2 float64
    var sumCV, sumXCV float64


    for _, s := range summaries {
        x := s.Date.Sub(baseTime).Hours() / 24.0
        sumX += x
        sumTIR += s.TIRPct
        sumXTIR += x * s.TIRPct
        sumX2 += x * x
        sumCV += s.CVPct
        sumXCV += x * s.CVPct
    }


    denom := n*sumX2 - sumX*sumX
    tirSlope, cvSlope := 0.0, 0.0
    if math.Abs(denom) > 1e-10 {
        tirSlope = (n*sumXTIR - sumX*sumTIR) / denom
        cvSlope = (n*sumXCV - sumX*sumCV) / denom
    }


    return CGMTrend{
        TIRTrend:       classifyTrend(tirSlope, 0.5),  // >0.5%/day = improving
        TIRSlopePerDay: math.Round(tirSlope*1000) / 1000,
        CVTrend:        classifyTrend(-cvSlope, 0.3),   // negative CV slope = improving
        CVSlopePerDay:  math.Round(cvSlope*1000) / 1000,
        DataPoints:     len(summaries),
    }
}


func classifyTrend(slope, threshold float64) string {
    if slope > threshold {
        return "IMPROVING"
    }
    if slope < -threshold {
        return "DETERIORATING"
    }
    return "STABLE"
}
```

```go
// kb-23-decision-cards/internal/services/cgm_card_rules.go
package services


import "fmt"


// CGMCardInput holds CGM metrics needed for card evaluation.
type CGMCardInput struct {
    TIRPct         float64
    TBRL1Pct       float64
    TBRL2Pct       float64
    TARL1Pct       float64
    TARL2Pct       float64
    CVPct          float64
    GRI            float64
    GRIZone        string
    GMI            float64
    LabHbA1c       *float64
    SufficientData bool
    CoveragePct    float64
    ActiveMeds     []string
    NocturnalHypos int
}


// CGMCard represents a decision card generated from CGM analytics.
type CGMCard struct {
    CardType   string   `json:"card_type"`
    Urgency    string   `json:"urgency"`
    Title      string   `json:"title"`
    Rationale  string   `json:"rationale"`
    Actions    []string `json:"actions"`
    Metrics    map[string]float64 `json:"metrics"`
}


// EvaluateCGMCards generates decision cards from CGM analytics.
func EvaluateCGMCards(input *CGMCardInput) []CGMCard {
    var cards []CGMCard


    // 0. Data quality check — must come first
    if !input.SufficientData {
        cards = append(cards, CGMCard{
            CardType: "CGM_DATA_QUALITY",
            Urgency:  "ROUTINE",
            Title:    "CGM Data Coverage Below Threshold",
            Rationale: fmt.Sprintf("CGM coverage %.0f%% is below the 70%% minimum required for reliable metrics. "+
                "Encourage more frequent scanning (isCGM) or check sensor adhesion.", input.CoveragePct),
            Actions: []string{
                "Review CGM scanning frequency with patient",
                "Check sensor adhesion and placement",
                "Consider real-time CGM if patient consistently under-scans",
            },
        })
        return cards // don't generate clinical cards from insufficient data
    }


    // 1. Hypoglycaemia risk — highest priority safety card
    tbrTotal := input.TBRL1Pct + input.TBRL2Pct
    if input.TBRL2Pct > 1.0 {
        urgency := "URGENT"
        if input.TBRL2Pct > 3.0 {
            urgency = "IMMEDIATE"
        }
        // Check for hypo-prone medications
        hasHypoMed := false
        for _, med := range input.ActiveMeds {
            if med == "SULFONYLUREA" || med == "INSULIN" {
                hasHypoMed = true
                break
            }
        }
        if hasHypoMed {
            urgency = "IMMEDIATE" // hypo-prone med + actual hypo = immediate
        }


        actions := []string{"Review medication regimen for hypoglycaemia risk"}
        if hasHypoMed {
            actions = append(actions, "Consider reducing sulfonylurea/insulin dose",
                "Evaluate switch to DPP4i or GLP-1 RA (lower hypo risk)")
        }
        actions = append(actions, "Set CGM low glucose alert at 80 mg/dL for early warning")


        cards = append(cards, CGMCard{
            CardType: "HYPOGLYCAEMIA_RISK",
            Urgency:  urgency,
            Title:    "Significant Time Below Range Detected",
            Rationale: fmt.Sprintf("TBR Level 2 (severe, <54 mg/dL): %.1f%% (target <1%%). "+
                "TBR Level 1 (<70 mg/dL): %.1f%% (target <4%%). "+
                "Total time below range: %.1f%%. CV %.1f%% indicates %s glucose.",
                input.TBRL2Pct, input.TBRL1Pct, tbrTotal,
                input.CVPct, cvLabel(input.CVPct)),
            Actions: actions,
            Metrics: map[string]float64{
                "tbr_l2_pct": input.TBRL2Pct, "tbr_l1_pct": input.TBRL1Pct,
                "cv_pct": input.CVPct,
            },
        })
    } else if tbrTotal > 4.0 {
        cards = append(cards, CGMCard{
            CardType: "HYPOGLYCAEMIA_RISK",
            Urgency:  "ROUTINE",
            Title:    "Elevated Time Below Range",
            Rationale: fmt.Sprintf("TBR total %.1f%% exceeds 4%% target. Monitor trend.", tbrTotal),
            Actions:  []string{"Review timing of hypoglycaemic episodes in AGP",
                "Consider medication timing adjustment"},
        })
    }


    // 2. Sustained hyperglycaemia
    if input.TARL2Pct > 5.0 {
        cards = append(cards, CGMCard{
            CardType: "SUSTAINED_HYPERGLYCAEMIA",
            Urgency:  "URGENT",
            Title:    "Excessive Time Above 250 mg/dL",
            Rationale: fmt.Sprintf("TAR Level 2 (>250 mg/dL): %.1f%% (target <5%%). "+
                "GRI Zone %s (%.0f). Therapy intensification indicated.",
                input.TARL2Pct, input.GRIZone, input.GRI),
            Actions: []string{
                "Review post-meal glucose patterns in AGP",
                "Consider therapy intensification",
                "Evaluate medication adherence",
                "Assess dietary carbohydrate intake",
            },
            Metrics: map[string]float64{
                "tar_l2_pct": input.TARL2Pct, "gri": input.GRI,
            },
        })
    }


    // 3. Low TIR without specific hypo/hyper emergency
    if input.TIRPct < 50.0 && input.TBRL2Pct <= 1.0 && input.TARL2Pct <= 5.0 {
        cards = append(cards, CGMCard{
            CardType: "LOW_TIME_IN_RANGE",
            Urgency:  "ROUTINE",
            Title:    "Time in Range Below Target",
            Rationale: fmt.Sprintf("TIR %.1f%% (target >70%%). GMI %.1f%%. "+
                "Predominant issue: %s.", input.TIRPct, input.GMI, predominantIssue(input)),
            Actions: []string{
                "Review AGP profile for actionable patterns",
                "Identify peak glucose times for targeted intervention",
            },
        })
    }


    // 4. Glucose variability
    if input.CVPct > 36.0 {
        cards = append(cards, CGMCard{
            CardType: "GLUCOSE_VARIABILITY",
            Urgency:  "ROUTINE",
            Title:    "Labile Glucose Pattern Detected",
            Rationale: fmt.Sprintf("CV %.1f%% exceeds 36%% stability threshold. "+
                "Labile glucose is an independent predictor of hypoglycaemia risk "+
                "and indicates unstable glycaemic control regardless of mean glucose.", input.CVPct),
            Actions: []string{
                "Prioritize variability reduction over mean glucose lowering",
                "Consider basal insulin optimization over bolus intensification",
                "Evaluate meal consistency and timing",
                "Screen for gastroparesis if pattern is irregular",
            },
        })
    }


    // 5. Nocturnal hypoglycaemia
    if input.NocturnalHypos > 0 {
        cards = append(cards, CGMCard{
            CardType: "NOCTURNAL_HYPOGLYCAEMIA",
            Urgency:  "URGENT",
            Title:    "Nocturnal Hypoglycaemia Detected",
            Rationale: fmt.Sprintf("%d nocturnal hypo event(s) detected in current period. "+
                "Nocturnal hypoglycaemia increases cardiac arrhythmia risk.", input.NocturnalHypos),
            Actions: []string{
                "Review bedtime snack routine",
                "Adjust evening/bedtime insulin dose",
                "Set CGM predictive low alert if available",
            },
        })
    }


    // 6. GMI-HbA1c discrepancy
    if input.LabHbA1c != nil {
        diff := input.GMI - *input.LabHbA1c
        if diff > 0.5 || diff < -0.5 {
            cards = append(cards, CGMCard{
                CardType: "GMI_HBAIC_DISCREPANCY",
                Urgency:  "ROUTINE",
                Title:    "GMI and Lab HbA1c Discrepancy",
                Rationale: fmt.Sprintf("GMI %.1f%% vs Lab HbA1c %.1f%% (difference %.1f%%). "+
                    "Investigate potential causes: hemoglobin variant, anemia, "+
                    "recent glycaemic change not yet reflected in HbA1c.",
                    input.GMI, *input.LabHbA1c, diff),
                Actions: []string{
                    "Check for hemoglobin variants (HbS, HbC, HbE)",
                    "Screen for iron deficiency or hemolytic anemia",
                    "Compare trend — recent improvement or deterioration?",
                },
            })
        }
    }


    return cards
}


func cvLabel(cv float64) string {
    if cv <= 36.0 {
        return "stable"
    }
    return "unstable/labile"
}


func predominantIssue(input *CGMCardInput) string {
    if input.TARL1Pct+input.TARL2Pct > input.TBRL1Pct+input.TBRL2Pct {
        return "hyperglycaemia (excessive time above range)"
    }
    return "hypoglycaemia (excessive time below range)"
}
```
